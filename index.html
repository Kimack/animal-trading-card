Indian rhinoceros

Rhino horns, including indian rhino horns, are made of keratin, which is also the key component of human hair and fingernails.

  • Scientific Name: Rhinoceros unicornis
  • Average Length: 2 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 35-50 years
  • Habitat: Dense rainforests and many others

The Indian rhinoceros, or greater one-horned rhinoceros, (Rhinoceros unicornis) has a single horn 20 to 100 cm long. It is nearly as large as the African white rhino. Its thick, silver-brown skin forms huge folds all over its body. Its upper legs and shoulders are covered in wart-like bumps, and it has very little body hair. Grown males are larger than females in the wild, weighing from 2,500–3,200 kg (5,500–7,100 lb). Shoulder height is 1.75–2.0 m (5.7–6.6 ft). Females weigh about 1,900 kg and are 3–4 m long.
